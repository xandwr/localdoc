# Funding

> Doctown is funded by people who’d rather build public tech than complain about private ones.

Look, if a thousand of us throw five bucks in the same hat, we can build the kind of infrastructure no VC would ever touch.
No dark patterns. No “engagement funnels.” No ads whispering that you’re the product.

Just clean, permanent, human-owned technology.

---

Every dollar goes somewhere visible. A slice keeps the servers humming. A slice keeps me alive long enough to keep building.
The rest goes right back into the commons—funding doc archiving, contributor bounties, and open tools that everyone can use.

You don’t buy perks here. You buy permanence—a guarantee that the tools you rely on tomorrow will still exist, unbought and unbroken, years from now.

It’s simple:
- No investors to please.
- No lock-in schemes.
- No exit plan—just an exist plan.

If we do this right, Doctown becomes proof that a small group of stubborn builders can outlast an entire wave of corporate decay.

Choose permanence over perks. Choose a future that doesn’t delete itself when funding dries up.
